<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and the Way of the Samurai">

Combat in this installment marks a departure from the "Soulslike" trend, focusing instead on precision, parrying, and environmental awareness. Musashi’s primary tool is his katana, but the depth of the system lies in how players interact with enemy stamina and positioning. The "steering" mechanic is particularly noteworthy; by holding a parry, Musashi can physically redirect an opponent's momentum, forcing them into hazards or off balance.

| Combat Move | Mechanic Type | Player Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Strike** | Offensive | Basic damage, chips away at enemy stamina. |
| **Prolonged Parry** | Defensive/Tactical | Allows "steering" enemies toward hazards. |
| **Guard Stance** | Defensive | Blocks attacks from all directions at the cost of stamina. |
| **Break Issen** | Execution | Instantly kills standard enemies via dismemberment. |
| **Combat Buffs** | Passive | Granted after successful consecutive parries or dodges. |

The environment is not just a backdrop. Players can flip tables to create temporary cover or use the narrow alleys of Kyoto to funnel supernatural threats. This tactical layer is essential when facing rival swordsmen who possess their own parry and counter-movements.

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Relying too heavily on the Guard Stance will deplete your stamina quickly. In boss fights, a depleted stamina bar leaves Musashi vulnerable to unblockable "Oni" strikes that can end a run instantly.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Oni Gauntlet and Soul Harvesting">

Central to the progression is the sentient gauntlet Musashi wears. This artifact absorbs the souls of defeated demons, categorized into three distinct colors. Managing these resources is the difference between surviving a linear dungeon and being overwhelmed by the supernatural forces of the Edo period.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Yellow Souls">
    - **Function**: Health Recovery
    - **Source**: Dropped by enemies or generated by hitting foes with Oni Armaments.
    - **Priority**: High in boss encounters.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Red Souls">
    - **Function**: Currency for Upgrades
    - **Source**: Standard drops from all defeated enemies.
    - **Priority**: Essential for long-term power scaling.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Blue Souls">
    - **Function**: Oni Energy
    - **Source**: Rare drops or specific environmental triggers.
    - **Priority**: Powers "Oni Armaments" for massive burst damage.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

The "Oni Vision" ability further enhances the gauntlet's utility. By activating this mode, Musashi can see through demonic illusions, identifying the true forms of enemies hiding in human crowds or locating hidden caches of Red Souls behind destructible walls.

| Gauntlet Feature | Resource Used | Combat Application |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Oni Armaments** | Blue Souls | Summons spectral weapons for heavy damage. |
| **Oni Vision** | Passive/Active | Detects hidden demons and secret items. |
| **Soul Absorption** | Automatic/Manual | Pulls in souls from a distance after kills. |
| **Upgrade System** | Red Souls | Enhances blade sharpness and gauntlet capacity. |

<Callout type="success" title="Boss Strategy">
When using Break Issen on a boss, you are presented with a choice: deal massive instant damage or extract a high volume of souls. If your health is low, opting for the soul extraction to find Yellow Souls is often the safer play.
</Callout>
