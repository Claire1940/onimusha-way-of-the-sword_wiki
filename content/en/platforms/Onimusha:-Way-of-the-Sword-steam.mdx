<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Difficulty">
**Onimusha: Way of the Sword** redefines its combat system, moving closer to a Dark Souls-like experience where precision and aggression are paramount. Unlike previous titles, this iteration feels significantly harder, demanding players master parries, dodges, and strategic attacks.

| Combat Aspect       | Description                                                 | Impact on Gameplay                                     |
|----------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| **Stamina System**   | Enemies have a yellow stamina bar and a red health bar.     | Depleting stamina stuns them, allowing for heavy damage. |
| **Aggressive Play**  | Faster kills mean less damage taken.                        | Encourages constant offense and quick decision-making. |
| **Parrying**         | Every successful parry is crucial.                          | Essential for mitigating damage and creating openings.  |
| **Healing Potions**  | Situational and sometimes clunky to use.                   | Adds an extra layer of challenge, requires practice.    |
| **Break System**     | Player stamina drops when taking damage.                    | Getting surrounded can lead to instant death.          |

The game features two basic attack styles: one-handed and two-handed. While the demo doesn't fully showcase the utility of two-handed attacks, aggressive one-handed combos, parries, and flanking maneuvers are highly effective. Environmental interactions, like picking up shields or smashing pots, offer minor tactical advantages.

<Callout type="warning" title="Defensive Play is Discouraged">
Attempting to play **Onimusha: Way of the Sword** defensively, relying heavily on blocks and parries, is not the intended strategy. The game actively encourages aggressive, in-your-face combat to break enemy stances and deal maximum damage.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the Aggressive Combat Flow">
Success in **Onimusha: Way of the Sword** hinges on understanding and mastering its aggressive combat flow. This isn't a game where you can patiently chip away at enemies; it rewards bold, calculated risks.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Aggression">
    Always seek to engage enemies quickly and relentlessly. The faster you incapacitate or defeat them, the less opportunity they have to retaliate. Use a mix of one-handed attacks and special abilities to maintain pressure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Target Stamina (Yellow Bar)">
    Focus on depleting the enemy's yellow stamina bar first. Once their stamina is broken, they become vulnerable to devastating attacks, often leading to quick kills or significant health reduction. This is key to managing groups and strong foes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master Parries and Dodges">
    While aggression is favored, precise parries are critical for deflecting attacks and creating openings. Use dodges to reposition and avoid unblockable attacks, especially when facing multiple enemies or powerful bosses.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Your Own Stamina">
    Be aware of your character's stamina. Taking damage reduces your stamina, which can leave you vulnerable. Avoid getting surrounded, and if your stamina is low, create space to recover before re-engaging.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Utilize Soul Absorption">
    The game features various souls you can absorb to replenish health, stamina, or special attack energy. The game's balance encourages charging back in even at low health, as hitting enemies can provide the souls you need most.
  </Step>
</Steps>
