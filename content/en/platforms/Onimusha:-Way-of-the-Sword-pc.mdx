### The 'Break' System Explained

The 'break' system is a core combat mechanic where enemies have both a **yellow stamina bar** and a **red health bar**. Reducing an enemy's stamina to zero often leaves them vulnerable to devastating attacks or instant kills. This system also applies to the player, making situational awareness critical in multi-enemy encounters.

| Combat Mechanic     | Description                                               | Player Impact                                               | Enemy Impact                                                |
| :------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Stamina (Yellow)**| Depletes with successful attacks; recovers over time.     | Player stamina drops when taking damage.                    | Reduces to zero for stagger/vulnerability.                  |
| **Health (Red)**    | Primary health bar.                                       | Depletes upon taking damage; replenished by healing items.  | Depletes upon taking damage; leads to defeat.               |
| **Parry**           | Timed block that can stagger enemies.                     | Requires precise timing; can leave player vulnerable if missed. | Effective against most attacks, creating openings.           |
| **Dodge**           | Quick movement to avoid attacks.                          | Essential for avoiding damage; consumes some stamina.       | Enemies have various attack patterns requiring different dodges. |
| **Aggression**      | Continuous attacking to prevent enemy recovery.          | Rewards with faster kills and less damage taken.            | Prevents enemies from regrouping or launching strong attacks. |

### Combat Styles and Tactics

The game offers both one-handed and two-handed attacks. While the demo suggested that one-handed attacks were often sufficient, the full game might introduce enemies or scenarios where the heavier two-handed strikes become necessary.

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the 'Break' System">
