<Section icon={HardDrive} title="Storage and Traversal Requirements">

One of the most critical aspects of the **Onimusha Way of the Sword system requirements** is the mandatory use of an **SSD**. Modern action games have moved away from traditional HDDs due to the slow data transfer rates that cause "pop-in" or long loading screens between areas. In *Onimusha: Way of the Sword*, the transition between the serene Kiyomizu-dera Temple and the twisted, Malice-infested versions of Kyoto happens rapidly.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Performance Optimization and Setup">

If your PC is hovering right at the edge of the minimum **Onimusha Way of the Sword system requirements**, you can take several steps to ensure the game remains responsive. Since the combat centers on timing-based defense (Parry and Deflect), even a small amount of input lag can be detrimental to your progression.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Update Graphics Drivers">
    Ensure you are using the latest "Game Ready" drivers from NVIDIA or AMD. These often contain specific optimizations for new Capcom titles that can improve frame stability by 5-10%.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enable Game Mode">
    On Windows 11, ensure **Game Mode** is toggled on. This prioritizes the game's process and prevents background Windows updates from siphoning CPU cycles during your duel with Sasaki Ganryu.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Adjust Shadow and Particle Settings">
    If you experience drops during **Oni Armament** use, lower the particle quality first. These effects are visually stunning but are the most taxing on the GPU's compute units.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Verify SSD Health">
    Since an SSD is required, ensure your drive has at least 15-20% free space. SSDs can slow down significantly when nearly full, which might lead to loading stutters in Mount Oe.
  </Step>
</Steps>
