<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Adjust Resolution">
    Lowering the in-game resolution from native 1280x800 to 720p can significantly improve frame rates without a major visual hit on the smaller screen. Experiment to find your preferred balance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Graphic Presets">
    Start with a "Medium" graphic preset and gradually increase individual settings like texture quality or shadow detail until you notice performance drops. "Low" settings will ensure maximum battery life and stable frame rates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Frame Rate Limiter">
    Utilize SteamOS's built-in frame rate limiter. Setting it to 30 FPS can provide a more consistent experience and save battery, especially if a stable 60 FPS is hard to maintain.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Shader Pre-Caching">
    Ensure shader pre-caching is enabled in SteamOS settings. This helps prevent stuttering during gameplay as the system compiles shaders in advance.
  </Step>
</Steps>
