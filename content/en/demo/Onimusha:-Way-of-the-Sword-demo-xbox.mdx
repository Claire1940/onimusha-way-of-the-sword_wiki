<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and the Break System">

Combat in the **Onimusha: Way of the Sword demo xbox** experience centers on the "Break System," a dual-bar mechanic that governs every encounter. Every enemy possesses a red health bar and a yellow stamina bar. Unlike traditional games where your own attacks consume your stamina, this system focuses on reducing the opponent's yellow bar through relentless aggression. Once an enemy's stamina is depleted, they become vulnerable to devastating "Issen" strikes or instant kills.

The weight of the combat is kinetic and thick, emphasized by sound design and visual feedback. Players can choose between one-handed quick strikes and two-handed heavy attacks. While the demo primarily showcases the efficiency of the one-handed style for parrying, the two-handed stance is essential for breaking the guard of heavily armored lizard-men and shielded humanoids.

| Attack Type | Button (Xbox) | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **One-Handed Strike** | X | Fast combos, building aggression, parrying |
| **Two-Handed Strike** | Y | Heavy damage, breaking guard, stagger |
| **Soul Absorption** | B (Hold) | Drawing in released souls for resources |
| **Oni Armament** | RB + X | Executing special energy attacks |
| **Healing Potion** | D-Pad Up | Restoring health (requires standing still) |

<Callout type="warning" title="Defensive Risk">
Blocking is significantly less effective than parrying. If you remain defensive for too long, enemies will break your stamina bar, leading to a near-instant death. Always look for the opening to counter-attack.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Enemy Analysis and Boss Strategy">

The **Onimusha: Way of the Sword demo xbox** version features a progression of enemy difficulty that tests your mastery of the parry mechanic. You begin against "Zombies"—humanoid husks that are easy to stagger. However, the difficulty spikes quickly with the introduction of armored lizard warriors and heavy sword-masters who possess multiple health bars and high resistance to stagger.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Clear the Perimeter">
    In the Kiyomizu-dera stage, prioritize the snipers on the hills. Their arrows can interrupt your combos and break your stamina at critical moments.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Parry">
    Against heavy sword-masters, wait for the glint of their blade. A successful parry instantly reduces 50% of their yellow stamina bar.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Boss: Sasaki Ganryu">
    Musashi's rival is fast and uses a talking gauntlet of his own. Stay inside his hitbox and avoid backing away, as his long-range thrusts are his most dangerous moves.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Enemy Type | Danger Level | Recommended Tactic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sword Zombie** | Low | Spam Square / X for quick kills |
| **Armored Lizard** | Medium | Use Y / Triangle to break armor |
| **Heavy Master** | High | Focus on parrying and Issen strikes |
| **Sasaki Ganryu** | Extreme | Stay aggressive, dodge toward his back |

<Callout type="warning" title="Healing Lag">
Healing potions in the demo have a noticeable delay. You cannot heal while moving or during an attack animation. Ensure you have created distance or staggered the enemy before attempting to use a potion.
</Callout>
