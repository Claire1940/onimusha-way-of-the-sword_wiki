<Section icon={Target} title="Core Gameplay Mechanics on Xbox">
**Onimusha: Way of the Sword** is an action-adventure game played from a third-person perspective, with a strong emphasis on sword combat. Musashi's arsenal includes various sword techniques, from basic strikes to parries and projectile deflection. A key mechanic, "Break Issen," allows players to instantly execute enemies by dismembering them after depleting their stamina. The game also introduces a sentient gauntlet that absorbs souls from defeated enemies, providing different benefits.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat Strategies and Musashi's Abilities">
Mastering combat in **Onimusha: Way of the Sword** requires understanding Musashi's unique abilities and the enemy's weaknesses. The game presents a variety of foes, from basic "zombies with swords" to heavily armored lizard-men and powerful sword-wielding brutes. Each enemy type demands a slightly different approach, though aggression remains a constant. The "Break Issen" system is central to combat, applying to both Musashi and his enemies. If Musashi's stamina (yellow bar) is broken, it can lead to devastating consequences, often an instant kill.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Parry">
    Learn the timing for parries to open enemies up for attacks and protect yourself. A prolonged parry can even "steer" enemies, causing them to fall in a specific direction, useful for crowd control.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize Break Issen">
    Focus on depleting enemy stamina to zero to trigger "Break Issen." For bosses, this deals massive damage or grants more souls, rather than an instant kill.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage Souls with the Gauntlet">
    Musashi's sentient gauntlet absorbs three types of souls: **Yellow** for health, **Red** for upgrades, and **Blue** for "Oni Armaments." Use Oni Armaments strategically for high damage, as they also replenish yellow souls.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Environmental Awareness">
    Use the environment to your advantage, such as flipping tables for temporary shields or smashing pots. In chaotic fights, positioning yourself against a wall can prevent enemies from breaking your guard from multiple sides.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Adapt to Enemy Types">
    Weak enemies are quickly dispatched, but stronger foes with more health and resistance to stagger require more focused aggression and parry timing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Soul Management">
The game's soul system is dynamic. When Musashi's health is low, defeated enemies tend to drop more health souls. If special attack energy is low, hitting enemies will yield more energy. This encourages players to stay aggressive even when in a precarious state.
</Callout>

</Section>
